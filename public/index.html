Stop Bullying — Stand Together

Bullying is a behavior where someone, alone or in a group, intentionally tries to hurt another person physically or emotionally. It is often repeated and involves a power imbalance. [oai_citation:14‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Types of bullying include:

  • Physical: hitting, pushing, kicking, pinching. [oai_citation:15‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Verbal: teasing, threatening, spreading rumors. [oai_citation:16‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Relational: ignoring, excluding someone from a group. [oai_citation:17‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Property: destroying or hiding someone’s belongings. [oai_citation:18‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Cyber: using social media, posting hurtful messages or videos. [oai_citation:19‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Bullying affects not only the victim, but also the bully and those who witness it. [oai_citation:20‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Victims may feel powerless, lose self-esteem, suffer from anxiety or depression, or even consider self-harm. [oai_citation:21‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Bullies themselves may act out due to low self-esteem, stress, or the need for control and recognition. [oai_citation:22‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Bystanders — those who see bullying happen — can unintentionally encourage it. Some learn that “power means respect,” and this cycle grows. [oai_citation:23‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

How to Help & Prevent Bullying

Everyone — both children and adults — must understand that bullying is serious. It’s not just “kids being kids.” [oai_citation:24‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

  • Listen to children who report being bullied. Encourage them. Don’t dismiss them as “telling tales.” [oai_citation:25‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Ask about what happened: who, where, how. Use this to plan support. [oai_citation:26‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Teach assertiveness: help children say, “I don’t like how you treat me.” [oai_citation:27‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • If bullying continues: convene talks with everyone involved — victims, bullies, and witnesses — plus parents and teachers. [oai_citation:28‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Promote “buddy systems”: encourage children to support each other so no one is alone. [oai_citation:29‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Foster empathy: encourage children to consider how others feel. “If that were me …” helps build understanding. [oai_citation:30‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • For children with emotional or behavioral struggles (like low self-esteem or ADHD), connect them to a mental health professional. [oai_citation:31‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Caring for Yourself (When You’re Being Bullied)

  • Avoid high-risk environments if possible. Stay safe. [oai_citation:32‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Use “I‑messages” to express how you feel. E.g., “When you say that, I feel hurt.” [oai_citation:33‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Record evidence: save messages, take pictures, write down what happened. [oai_citation:34‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Tell someone you trust. Share what you’re going through, don’t suffer alone. [oai_citation:35‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Remember: being bullied is not your fault. You deserve respect and help. [oai_citation:36‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • If things get too hard — talk to a psychologist, psychiatrist, or counselor. [oai_citation:37‡manarom.com](https://www.manarom.com/blog/stop_bullying.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
