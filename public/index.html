What is Bullying?

Bullying is intentional harmful behavior that is repeated over time and involves an imbalance of power. It can take many forms:

Physical Bullying

Hitting, pushing, or damaging property

Verbal Bullying

Name-calling, threats, spreading rumors

Social Bullying

Exclusion, embarrassing someone publicly

Cyberbullying

Online harassment, sharing private information

The Impact of Bullying

On Victims

Low self-esteem, anxiety, depression, school avoidance, and in severe cases, self-harm or suicidal thoughts.

On Bullies

Increased risk of substance abuse, criminal behavior, and difficulty maintaining relationships later in life.

On Bystanders

Feelings of guilt, fear of becoming targets themselves, and increased anxiety about school or work environments.

How You Can Help

If You're Being Bullied

  • Remember it's not your fault - no one deserves to be bullied
  • Tell a trusted adult about what's happening
  • Stay near adults or groups of friends when possible
  • Practice confident body language and responses
  • Document incidents with dates, times, and details

If You Witness Bullying

  • Speak up if it's safe - say "That's not cool" or "Leave them alone"
  • Support the person being bullied - walk away with them
  • Report the incident to an authority figure
  • Include the person being bullied in your activities
  • Set a positive example with your own behavior
